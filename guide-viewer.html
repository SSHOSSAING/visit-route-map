Opening Routes

When someone shares a route map with you, opening it is simple and requires no account or installation.

📧

From Email

Tap the link in your email app. The map opens directly in your browser.

💬

From Messaging

Tap the link in WhatsApp, Teams, Slack, or any messaging app.

🌐

From Browser

Paste the link into any web browser address bar and press Enter.

i
No installation needed
Visit Route Map runs entirely in your web browser. There is nothing to download, install, or sign up for.
★
Bookmark for quick access
Save the route link to your browser bookmarks or home screen for one-tap access during your visit.

Reading the Map

Route maps display your visit itinerary as numbered markers connected by a route line. Understanding the visual elements helps you follow the plan efficiently.

Route Markers

Each stop on the route is shown as a numbered circular marker on the map.

  • Numbers indicate the visit order — follow them sequentially
  • Marker color may vary by camp or agency (if the creator configured this)
  • Clustered markers zoom in to separate overlapping stops

Pop-up Details

Tap or click any marker to open a detailed pop-up with information about that stop.

  • Time — scheduled arrival time for this stop
  • Duration — estimated time spent at this location
  • Activity — description of what happens at this stop
  • Camp — the camp or site name
  • Agency — the organization or partner responsible
i
Route line
The colored line connecting markers shows the suggested travel path between stops. It is a visual guide, not turn-by-turn navigation.

Map Controls

The map includes several interactive controls to help you explore the route and orient yourself.

Layer Switcher

Change the base map style using the layer switcher button (usually in the top-right corner).

  • Street — detailed roads, landmarks, and labels
  • Satellite — aerial imagery for visual context
  • Terrain — elevation and topographic features

Live Location Tracking

See your current position on the map in real time.

  1. Tap the location button (square icon, usually under the layer switcher)
  2. Allow the browser's location permission prompt
  3. A blue dot appears showing your current position
  4. The dot updates automatically as you move
!
Location accuracy
Accuracy depends on your device's GPS signal and network connection. Indoor locations may show reduced precision. Ensure GPS/location services are enabled on your device.
★
Privacy note
Your location is processed entirely on your device. It is not stored, transmitted, or shared with anyone — including the route creator.

Zoom and Pan

  • Scroll wheel or pinch to zoom in and out
  • Click and drag or swipe to pan the map
  • Double-tap to zoom in on a specific area

Mobile Usage

Route maps are fully responsive and work seamlessly on smartphones and tablets.

📱

Collapsible Title

On small screens, the route title collapses. Tap the ▾ arrow to expand and see the full title, subtitle, and date.

ℹ️

Route Summary

Tap the round ⓘ button on the map to view the route summary and disclaimer.

📍

Location Button

The square location tracking button sits under the layer switcher for easy thumb access.

★
Add to home screen
On iOS: tap Share → Add to Home Screen. On Android: tap Menu → Add to Home screen. This creates an app-like shortcut for instant access.

Touch Gestures

  • Single tap a marker to open its details
  • Two-finger pinch to zoom in and out
  • Single-finger drag to pan the map
  • Two-finger rotate (on some devices) to rotate the map view

Troubleshooting

Map does not load

  • Check your internet connection — the map tiles load from online servers
  • Try refreshing the page (pull down on mobile)
  • Disable any ad blockers or privacy extensions that might block map tiles

Location tracking not working

  • Ensure GPS/Location services are enabled in your device settings
  • Grant location permission when the browser prompts you
  • Try moving to an area with clear sky view for better GPS signal
  • Some browsers require HTTPS for geolocation — check the URL starts with https://

Markers are missing or in wrong places

  • Zoom out to see if markers are clustered together
  • Check the route title bar to confirm you opened the correct route
  • Contact the route creator if the data appears incorrect
i
Still having issues?
Contact the person who shared the route link with you. They can verify the route data and re-publish if needed.
